HUSKYKIOSK

Mission

The mission of this web application is to be an accessible resource to learn how one could pursue further education after incarceration. For those who haven't been incarcerated but want to learn about the divide between the general public and the stereotypic former-inmates, this is the resource for that!

Goals

Accessibility for Multiple Devices

While the internet is abundant with information, rarely do we see resources that cater towards different spectrums of devices. Some may only have access to smartphones, laptops, or tablets. However, the format of a website/application tends to be more fit towards one device, whereas we want to have this website scale to be seen through many diverse mediums

Intuitive Design

Considering the length of time people get out of prison varies, their inclination towards technology varies widely as well. Some people that haven't used a computer in 10 years or even those that have never used a smartphone will find it hard to navigate through an online resource if it's cluttered. For example, we've made sure that this site is intuitive by making the menu bar fixed to the top no matter where you scroll

Connect With Professionals

It would be wrong to assume our users are all in urban areas. While the assumption is that if you have a modern device, you would most likely be in an urban area, that is not the case. In urban areas, people can more easily find professionals to talk to for niche subjects. But what comes of the rural population? For the formerly-incarcerated, or users wanting to learn about the division between formerly-incarcerated and the public, we want to connect them to professionals that can help them with their questions and struggles. While they might not be able to meet face to face, we have the power to connect these professionals and our users through online chat, webcam, and more.
